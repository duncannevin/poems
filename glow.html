Glow
Once I looked at her, back when we were barely care-free, back when we each could go left or right — life was simple then, wake work repeat, our lives still distant, her breath still not known.
She radiated, that glow in a dark room. A pale glow, but a glow none the less. I saw her — she was different, a person the person I saw, and I fixated on her like a moth to flame.
Last night after supper I looked at her — oh that glow — how it's so bright now, her face so precious, beautiful, the only face I need.
Her glow only gets brighter year after year.
I will always be a moth fixated on that flame — oh how I love that woman.
